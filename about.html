About

Kenma Kozume, initially portrayed as disinterested in volleyball, finds himself unexpectedly entangled in a high-stakes match against Karasuno. His first encounter with Shōyō Hinata hints at a growing curiosity, even if he claims neutrality toward the sport. Despite Kenma's initial stance of only playing for his friend Kuroo, his interactions with Hinata and his analytical mind begin to draw him deeper into the competition. As Karasuno and Nekoma finally face each other at Nationals, long-standing anticipation builds between both teams and coaches, highlighting the emotional and historical significance of this matchup.

The match kicks off with intensity as both teams demonstrate sharp strategy and raw athleticism. Each rally becomes a mental and physical battle, with Kenma calculating every move and Hinata countering with unpredictable plays. Karasuno surprises Nekoma with their improved synchronized attacks and strategic adaptations, particularly with Hinata using his agility and new techniques to bypass blockers. Meanwhile, Kenma tries to suppress Hinata's momentum by targeting his movement and limiting his effectiveness on the court. The rivalry is less about animosity and more about mutual respect and growth.

Throughout the second set, Kenma's focus on outmaneuvering Hinata grows increasingly personal and thrilling. As Karasuno begins to adapt, Kenma feels both challenged and exhilarated—emotions he rarely associates with volleyball. Hinata’s perseverance pays off when he finally breaks through the triple block, marking a turning point. Both teams are pushed to their limits, and even typically reserved characters like Tsukishima and Yamaguchi show rare moments of emotional intensity. The second set ends with Karasuno winning, thanks to Hinata’s incredible defensive play, which stuns Nekoma and lifts his own team's morale.

As the final set begins, both teams reflect on their pasts and the bonds they’ve formed. Kenma’s backstory with Kuroo is explored, revealing how the sport became more than just an obligation—it became a shared passion. Despite exhaustion, both sides refuse to relent. Kenma, now fully invested, pushes past his limits and starts to understand the joy Hinata finds in volleyball. The match becomes less about winning and more about experiencing the full thrill of competition. In the final moments, Kenma tries to make one last play but fails due to sheer fatigue, and Karasuno wins the match.

After the match, the atmosphere is one of bittersweet exhaustion and deep gratitude. Kenma, despite the loss, admits how much fun he had, thanking Kuroo for bringing him into the sport. Both teams express mutual respect during their farewells, with promises of future rematches. Coaches, players, and even rivals share emotional moments, acknowledging the growth and effort of their respective teams. As the story ends, the scene shifts to new rivalries forming, with Hinata being challenged by Hoshiumi, signaling that the journey of competition and self-discovery continues beyond this unforgettable match.
